Malcom X Tribute Page

African American Revolutionary, Muslim Minister, Human Rights Activist

Here is a timeline of Malcolm X's life

  • 1925 - Born Malcolm Little in Omaha, Nebraska.
  • 1926 - Relocates to Milwaukee with his family because of Ku Klux Klan threats, then shortly after to Lansing, Michigan.
  • 1941 - Left mason High School in Mason, Michigan before graduating.
  • 1943- Malcom X moved to New York's Harlem neighbourhood, where he found employment on the New Haven Railroad and engaged in various crimes.
  • 1945- Returned to Boston.
  • 1946- He was arrested while picking up a stolen watch he had left at a shop for repairs, and in February began serving a sentence of eight to ten years at Charlestown State Prison for larceny and breaking and entering..
  • 1948- Malcom X was transferred to Norfolk Prison Colony. In the same year, he wrote to Elijah Muhammad, the leader of the Nation of Islam.
  • 1950- Began signing his name "Malcolm X".
  • 1952- He's granted parole in August.
  • 1953- Malcom X was named assistant minister of the Nation's Temple Number One in Detroit.
  • 1955- Continued his successful recruitment of members on behalf of the Nation of Islam.
  • 1958- Married Betty Sanders, and fathered 6 children with her.
  • 1957 - Malcolm X founded the newspaper for the Nation of Islam.
  • 1964 - Malcolm X made a pilgrimage to Mecca, or Hajj.
  • 1965 - Assassinated at the age of 39.

Malcom X is more than just an African American Revolutionary, muslim minister, and human rights activist, he is man who's life echoes the history and struggle of the black man.
